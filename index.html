Advisor: Dr Anand Narayanan

I am a PhD researcher studying the circumgalactic medium (CGM) and intergalactic medium (IGM) using quasar absorption line spectroscopy. My research focuses on understanding galaxy evolution through processes such as gas accretion, outflows, and feedback.

My work involves analysing quasar absorption systems using HST–COS spectroscopy and studying galaxy environments and kinematics using VLT–MUSE integral field spectroscopy to investigate galaxy–absorber connections.

Research Areas

Circumgalactic Medium and Intergalactic Medium

Studying the multiphase structure of the circumgalactic medium using quasar absorption spectroscopy in order to understand galaxy evolution, gas accretion, and feedback processes in galaxies.

Galaxy–Absorber Connections

Using VLT–MUSE integral field spectroscopy to identify galaxies associated with absorption systems and study galaxy kinematics and environments to determine whether absorbers trace inflows, outflows, or extended circumgalactic gas.

Current Research Projects

  • Project 1: Ne VIII absorbers in quasar spectra.
  • Project 2: Weak Mg II absorbers and their environments using large spectroscopic survey datasets.

Software Tools

Cosmology Calculator

A Flutter-based cosmological calculator implementing the ΛCDM cosmological model.

The app computes cosmological quantities such as:

  • Age of the Universe
  • Lookback Time
  • Comoving Distance
  • Angular Diameter Distance
  • Luminosity Distance
  • CMB Temperature scaling

It supports multiple universe models including flat ΛCDM, open universes, and general curvature cosmologies.
