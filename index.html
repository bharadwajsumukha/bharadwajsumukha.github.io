PhD Scholar – Astronomy and Astrophysics

Tracing the invisible threads that connect galaxies

Department of Earth and Space Sciences
Indian Institute of Space Science and Technology (IIST), Thiruvananthapuram
Advisor: Dr Anand Narayanan

I am a PhD researcher studying the circumgalactic medium (CGM) and intergalactic medium (IGM) using quasar absorption line spectroscopy. My research focuses on understanding galaxy evolution through processes such as gas accretion, outflows, and feedback.

My work involves analysing quasar absorption systems using HST–COS spectroscopy and studying galaxy environments and kinematics using VLT–MUSE integral field spectroscopy to investigate galaxy–absorber connections.

✉ Contact Me 📄 View CV

Research Areas

Circumgalactic Medium and Intergalactic Medium

Studying the circumgalactic medium using quasar absorption line spectroscopy to understand galaxy evolution, gas accretion, outflows, and feedback processes in galaxies.

Galaxy–Absorber Connections

Using VLT–MUSE integral field spectroscopy to identify galaxies associated with absorption systems and study galaxy kinematics and environments to determine whether absorbers trace inflows, outflows, or extended circumgalactic gas.

Research Experience

PhD Research – Circumgalactic and Intergalactic Medium

Indian Institute of Space Science and Technology (IIST), Thiruvananthapuram

Spectroscopic study of the Circumgalactic Medium (CGM) and Intergalactic Medium (IGM) using quasar absorption line systems, combining HST–COS spectroscopy with VLT–MUSE observations to investigate galaxy–absorber connections and the physical and kinematic properties of gas around galaxies, including Ne VIII, O VI, and Mg II absorbers at low redshift.

Dissertation Research Project

Indian Institute of Astrophysics (IIA)

Topic: Transiting Exoplanetary Atmospheres – Spectroscopic Reduction and Observational Techniques
Performed end-to-end spectroscopic reduction of ground-based observations of two hot Jupiter exoplanets (HAT-P1-b and WASP-33b) using IRAF, including bias subtraction, flat-field correction, aperture extraction, and wavelength calibration, followed by transit light curve fitting and MCMC parameter estimation using pylightcurve.

Project Assistant – COSMOS Outreach Program
